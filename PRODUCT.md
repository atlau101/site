# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Primary users are hiring managers, recruiters, and operators reviewing Andrew Lau's work quickly. Secondary users are peers, mentors, and collaborators who want to understand how he thinks through ambiguous problems. They usually arrive in evaluation mode, not browsing mode, and need to form a judgment from a few pages.

## Product Purpose
This portfolio exists to show how Andrew thinks, not just what he shipped. The site should make it easy to understand the problem context, the decision-making process, what worked, what failed, and what changed afterward. Success looks like a reviewer leaving with a clear sense of judgment, range, and honest self-awareness rather than a vague impression of polish.

## Brand Personality
Reflective, rigorous, candid.

The tone should feel like a smart field journal: analytical without sounding corporate, self-aware without becoming confessional, and confident enough to state where the work fell short.

## Anti-references
Do not make this feel like a startup landing page, a glossy agency portfolio, or an achievement scrapbook. Avoid generic personal-brand hype, overdesigned UI that competes with the writing, and case studies that flatten failures into tidy success stories.

## Design Principles
1. Let the thinking carry the page. Writing, evidence, and structure should do more work than decorative UI.
2. Preserve friction where it is useful. Honest tradeoffs, uncertainty, and failed paths should remain visible when they clarify judgment.
3. Keep the interface editorial and restrained. The design should frame the work like a memo or notebook, not like a marketing campaign.
4. Use consistency to build trust, then vary emphasis based on the project. Reflection-heavy projects can become more text-first without breaking the system.
5. Optimize for fast evaluation. A reviewer should understand the stakes, outputs, and main lesson of a project within a short scan.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target a solid WCAG AA baseline. Preserve readable contrast, comfortable long-form line lengths, keyboard-accessible interactions, and reduced-motion-safe behavior for animated elements. Content should remain understandable even when images or visuals are skipped.
